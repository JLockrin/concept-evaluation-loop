# Lateral alternatives: subscription tier

## Reframe

The real question isn't "subscription vs one-time" — it's "how do we increase predictable revenue and retention without alienating episodic buyers?"

## Alternatives

### A. Annual license with renewal nudge
**Idea:** Sell a 12-month license at a discount vs monthly subscription.
**How it achieves the outcome:** Front-loads revenue, reduces churn anxiety, feels less like "rent."
**Tradeoffs:** Less MRR smoothness; renewal still requires a decision point.
**First step:** Price annual at 10 months of hypothetical monthly rate.

### B. Membership with consumable credits
**Idea:** One-time purchase of credits; optional auto-refill subscription for power users.
**How it achieves the outcome:** Episodic buyers stay on credits; heavy users opt into recurring.
**Tradeoffs:** More complex to explain; needs usage metering.
**First step:** Identify top 10% usage patterns to size credit packs.

### C. Paid updates / maintenance plan
**Idea:** Core product stays one-time; charge annually for updates, support, and new content.
**How it achieves the outcome:** Separates asset purchase from ongoing service — familiar model (software maintenance).
**Tradeoffs:** Must deliver visible updates yearly; free riders on old versions.
**First step:** List what "maintenance year 2" would include.

## Recommended lateral path

**Annual license (A)** balances predictability with lower churn risk for a product still proving retention. Add auto-refill credits (B) later if power-user patterns emerge.

## Questions to unblock

- What % of customers return within 12 months today?
- Is ongoing value content, features, or support — or all three?
- What do competitors charge monthly vs annually?
