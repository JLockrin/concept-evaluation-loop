# Arguments for: subscription tier

## Summary

A subscription tier creates predictable recurring revenue, deepens customer relationships through ongoing value delivery, and aligns pricing with customers who want continuous access rather than episodic purchases.

## Arguments

### 1. Revenue predictability
Monthly recurring revenue smooths cash flow and makes forecasting more reliable than lumpy one-time sales.

### 2. Higher lifetime value
Subscribers who stay 6+ months typically generate more revenue than equivalent one-time buyers.

### 3. Ongoing engagement loop
Subscription naturally incentivizes shipping regular updates, which improves product quality for all users.

### 4. Competitive positioning
Many comparable products offer subscriptions; absence may signal a less mature or less committed product.

### 5. Upsell path
A low-tier subscription creates a runway to premium tiers, add-ons, or annual plans.

## Strongest single reason

Predictable MRR changes how you plan hiring, marketing spend, and roadmap bets — one-time revenue alone makes growth planning guesswork at small scale.

## What would need to be true

- Churn stays below ~5% monthly after the first 90 days
- Subscription delivers enough ongoing value to justify recurring payment
- One-time option remains for buyers who prefer it
- Billing and dunning are automated enough for a small team
