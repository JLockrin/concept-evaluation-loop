# Evaluation synthesis: subscription tier

**Eval ID:** `subscription-tier-2026-01-15`
**Recommendation:** Proceed with changes

## TL;DR

Don't launch a monthly subscription yet. Pilot an **annual license** first — it captures most MRR benefits with lower churn risk and simpler positioning. Keep one-time purchase as the default entry. Revisit monthly subscription after 12-month renewal data.

## Recommendation

**Verdict:** Proceed with changes

Launch an annual plan, not monthly subscription. The pro case for predictable revenue is strong, but con's churn objection is acute for episodic-value products. Lateral's annual license hybrid wins on risk-adjusted outcome.

## Options

### Option 1: Annual license pilot (recommended)
Price at ~10 months of target monthly rate. Offer alongside one-time. Measure renewal at month 11.

### Option 2: Monthly subscription (defer)
Revisit after annual pilot shows >40% renewal and clear ongoing value delivery capacity.

## If you proceed

1. Define "year 2 value" (updates, content, support) before pricing
2. Ship annual + one-time in Stripe with clear comparison table
3. Run 90-day pilot; track conversion split and support ticket volume

## If you defer or reject

Default to one-time + lateral option C (paid maintenance) if annual feels too aggressive.
