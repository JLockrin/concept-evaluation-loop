# Arguments against: subscription tier

## Summary

Adding subscriptions before product-market fit is solidified risks buyer confusion, support burden, and churn that damages brand trust — especially when the core value proposition is still episodic.

## Arguments

### 1. Cannibalization risk
Existing one-time buyers may expect subscription features for free, creating resentment and support tickets.

### 2. Churn sensitivity
A 5% monthly churn rate means losing half your base yearly — small teams can't outrun that with acquisition alone.

### 3. Value delivery pressure
Subscriptions demand continuous shipping; without capacity, you accumulate "zombie subscribers" who cancel at first renewal.

### 4. Pricing complexity
Two purchase models (one-time + subscription) increase checkout friction and confuse comparison shoppers.

### 5. Implementation cost
Stripe subscriptions, proration, failed payment recovery, and cancellation flows are non-trivial for a small team.

## Strongest single objection

If the product's core job is done in a single session or purchase, a subscription feels like rent-seeking — buyers will churn at month one and leave negative reviews.

## What would need to be true to proceed anyway

- Clear ongoing value proposition distinct from one-time purchase
- Automated dunning and self-serve cancellation
- Pricing where 12-month LTV exceeds one-time by 2x+
- Pilot with 10% of traffic before full launch
